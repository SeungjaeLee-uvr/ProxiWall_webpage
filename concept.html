01 Interaction Concept

Distance becomes
meaning.

ProxiWall maps familiar museum movement to a progressive information funnel: orient from afar, compare at mid-range, and commit through touch.

01

Overview

> 2.0 M · FAR

See the whole gallery.

From beyond two meters, the wall presents 8–10 artifacts at a glance. The goal is orientation: visitors scan the collection, understand spatial relationships, and decide where to go next.

8–10 artifactsGallery-level viewLow-gain navigation
02

Browsing

0.7–2.0 M · MID

Compare nearby works.

As a visitor approaches, the system reduces the set to 4–6 artifacts. Lateral body movement pans through candidates while a focus indicator keeps the active work legible.

4–6 artifactsCandidate comparison≤ 2 items / sec
03

Detail

≤ 0.7 M · NEAR

Reach out and select.

Within comfortable arm reach, ProxiWall reveals 1–3 detailed artifacts. A direct wall touch confirms the final selection with ripple and click feedback.

1–3 artifacts≥ 44 mm target≤ 100 ms feedback
